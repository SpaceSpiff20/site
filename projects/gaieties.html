Gaieties 2025

Director Stanford, CA September 2025 – November 2025

Key Achievements:

  • Directed a 25+ person cast through auditions, casting, rehearsal planning, and full production process
  • Built an ensemble-centered rehearsal culture through table work, improv exercises, and intentional community-building
  • Led character and performance coaching for leads (including first-time performers) using Uta Hagen–influenced acting pedagogy
  • Coordinated closely with assistant director + stage manager to schedule rehearsals around complex student availability and evolving material
  • Partnered with lighting and sound teams during tech to align cues and transitions with the show’s narrative and comedic pacing
  • Navigated scheduling disruptions and last-minute changes with an improv-driven mindset: “strong hits, lightly held”
  • Achieved strong audience outcomes: ~4,000 tickets sold, ~2% increase YoY, and sold out closing night
  • Helped foster sustained engagement: multiple cast members immediately pursued future leadership roles


    • Detailed Overview:
    As director of Stanford Gaieties, a student-written musical tradition dating back to 1911 and performed the week before Big Game,I led a 25+ person cast through an intensive fall rehearsal process to bring an original script to life.

    My directing approach balances a clear, script-faithful vision with generous room for actors to explore, play, and surprise me. We began with a full table read and ensemble-building work, then used improv exercises to strengthen presence and connection in scenes. I’m a firm believer that deep attention to scene partners, paired with a precise understanding of each character’s wants and goals, creates the most compelling (and funniest) stage work.

    Behind the scenes, I worked closely with my assistant director and stage manager to design rehearsal schedules around complex student availability and evolving material (a natural challenge in an entirely original production). I came into rehearsals with detailed blocking plans and diagrams, then refined them collaboratively. I also mentored performers throughout the process, including first-time actors in leading roles, using character work grounded in Uta Hagen–influenced acting pedagogy. (Yes, this included spending rehearsals studying bear physicality for our Oski the Bear.)

    During tech, I partnered with lighting and sound to walk scene-by-scene through cues and transitions, aligning design choices with the story we were telling. When inevitable obstacles came up, like sudden space issues or incomplete orchestration, I relied on an improv mindset I value deeply: strong hits, lightly held. I show up with a plan, then adapt quickly and calmly when circumstances change.

    The production was a major success, selling ~4,000 tickets, outperforming the previous year by ~2%, and selling out on closing night. What I’m proudest of, though, is the community the process built: several cast members immediately pursued future leadership roles (assistant producing, choreography, vocal direction). Ultimately, directing Gaieties affirmed something central for me, I feel most fulfilled when I get to be a conduit for other people’s creativity.
