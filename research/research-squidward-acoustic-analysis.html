Acoustic Analysis of Squidward Q Tentacles

Researcher and Author Stanford, CA April 2025 – June 2025

Abstract:
This paper quantifies and formalizes the acoustic and articulatory elements of Rodger Bumpass's performance as the character Squidward Q. Tentacles from "SpongeBob SquarePants". The investigation focuses on comparing formant frequencies, pitch, and spectral tilt of vowels articulated by Bumpass in his natural voice and in the Squidward voice, using samples pulled from online interviews. The study found significant and measurable phonetic differences. Key articulatory changes identified include an increase in the fundamental frequency (F0mean), the incorporation of a slight creakiness to the voice, and the most impactful difference, the partial lowering of the velum to add nasality to the voicing. The paper successfully identified and measured these differences and theorized the articulatory processes employed by the voice actor.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Formant Frequencies and Vowel Quality: Comparing formant frequencies (F1, F2, F3) to analyze vowel shifts, noting that F1 generally tends to increase and F3 tends to decrease in the Squidward voice.
  • Pitch (Fundamental Frequency - F0): Analyzing the average change in F0mean, which generally appears to increase, indicating a faster vibration rate of the vocal cords in the Squidward voice.
  • Voicing Type and Nasality: Investigating changes in the spectral tilt (H1-H2) and observing acoustic correlates of nasality, concluding that the Squidward voice incorporates a slight creakiness and, most significantly, a partial lowering of the velum to add nasality.
